### Build an Android APK

The dApp Publishing tool requires you to submit an Android APK of your app.

- If you already have an Android app (e.g. published to the Google Play Store), you can generate a release build APK.
- If you have a web app, follow [this guide](/recipes/general/publishing-a-web-app) to convert it to an Android app and generate an APK for submission.

<Note>
**NOTE**

Ensure you are submitting a release build of your app that is [signed](https://developer.android.com/studio/publish/app-signing#opt-out). Debug builds will not be accepted.

</Note>

### Test on an Android device

Thoroughly test your app to catch bugs and crashes before submitting.

You do not need a Solana Mobile device - test against any comparable Android device or emulator.

- If using Mobile Wallet Adapter, test your app's connect and signing flows with popular MWA wallets like Phantom and Solflare.
- Verify your sign-in and sign-out flows work end-to-end on the release build.
- If your app is gated, paid, or region-locked (e.g. beta access, NFT-gated), give App Review test credentials and step-by-step instructions to reach full access.

### Prepare your app listing page

Your listing page is the user's first impression. Prepare assets ahead of time, including your icon, screenshots/videos, and text content.

See the [Listing Page guidelines](/dapp-store/listing-page-guidelines) to visualize your app's listing page and learn best practices.

### Proceed to app submission

When you're ready, follow the [Submit a New App](/dapp-store/submit-new-app) guide. The process is self-service and can be started whenever you're ready.

After you submit, your app enters App Review - see [dApp Store Support](/dapp-store/support) for what happens next.

### App promotion

To promote your app after launch, see the [Marketing & Partnership docs](/marketing/overview).
